LGBTQ Survey Analysis

Project Overview

This project focused on supporting a Canadian non-profit organization by processing, cleaning, and visualizing participant survey data. The primary goal was to transform unstructured responses and inconsistent datasets into actionable insights, enabling the organization to make informed decisions and better engage stakeholders.

Methodology

The data was cleaned in Python by standardizing column names and grouping freeform survey responses into consistent categories using mapping dictionaries.

Open-ended feedback was analyzed by extracting keywords and identifying recurring themes, which were categorized into actionable insights.

Interactive dashboards were designed in Tableau to visually present key findings on demographics, participant satisfaction, and areas for improvement.

Visualizations

- Demographics Dashboard:
Bar Chart: Gender distribution highlights the participant breakdown with 49.9% identifying as women/girls and 45.1% as men/boys.
Relatable Attributes: Categories such as "Person of Colour" (32.7%) and "None of the above" (25.27%) showcase diversity and inclusivity within the program's reach.

- Feedback and Session Ratings:
Donut Chart: Overall satisfaction ratings show 59.6% of participants rated the session 4 or higher.
Stacked Bar Chart: Ratings across dimensions such as "Learning Consent" (54.2%) and "Consent Importance" (69.2%) provide a detailed breakdown of session impact.
Pie Chart: Recommendation results reveal 85.9% of participants would recommend the session

- Facilitator Feedback
Bar Chart: Facilitator performance ratings show "Question Handling" (67%) and "Engagement" (62.6%) as strong points.
Positive Feedback: Participants highlighted qualities like "Great Teaching" (77.5%) and "Kindness" (15.4%).
Improvement Suggestions: Common feedback included "Pace Adjustment" (32%) and "More Activities" (28%).

- Workshop Feedback and Suggestions
Donut Chart: Positive feedback metrics show 97% of participants expressed a positive tone, emphasizing clear communication and engaging activities.
Bar Chart: Improvement suggestions focus on areas like "More Activities" (50%), "Better Time Management" (21.7%), and "New Topics" (17.6%).
