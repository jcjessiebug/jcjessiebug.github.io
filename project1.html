Heart Disease Prediction

Overview

This project leverages data from the CDCâ€™s Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) to explore health and demographic factors contributing to heart disease. By applying advanced machine learning models, the analysis aims to identify key predictors of heart disease, enabling early intervention and data-driven healthcare strategies.

Dataset

The analysis utilized a dataset of over 240,000 records from the CDC's Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System, encompassing 40 variables. Notable variables include:

- Demographic factors: Age, sex, and state of residence.

- Health factors: Smoking status, general health, physical activity, and BMI.

- Medical history: History of angina and previous heart attacks.

Methodology

- Exploratory Analysis:Identified relationships between demographic and health factors (e.g., higher BMI and smoking were strongly associated with heart disease).

- Feature Selection: Variables like smoking, age, and angina were prioritized based on their contribution to heart disease risk.

- Modeling: Both Random Forest and XGBoost were implemented. XGBoost demonstrated the best accuracy (96.05%), while Random Forest provided a balanced trade-off between recall and precision.

Key Findings

- Critical Factors:Smoking status, general health, and age were the strongest predictors of heart disease.

- Model Insights: XGBoost outperformed Random Forest in speed and precision but at the expense of slightly lower recall. Random Forest, with balanced precision and recall, is more reliable for healthcare scenarios prioritizing recall.

- Geographic Insights: Certain U.S. states exhibited higher prevalence, suggesting localized risk factors like lifestyle or healthcare access disparities.
